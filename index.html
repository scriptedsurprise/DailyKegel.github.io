The Stop Test

The easiest way to identify your pelvic floor: Next time you urinate, try to stop the flow mid-stream. The muscles you feel are your pelvic floor. ⚠️ Only try this ONCE for identification — not as regular practice!

Locate Your Pelvic Floor

Your pelvic floor muscles form a sling between your pubic bone and tailbone. They support your bladder, bowel, and core. Let's find them together!

🛑

The Stop Test

Next time you urinate, try to stop the flow mid-stream. The muscles you engage are your pelvic floor muscles. You'll feel a lifting and squeezing deep inside.

⚠️ Important
Only try this ONCE for identification. Regularly stopping urine flow can cause urinary tract issues.

Squeeze as if holding back gas. Focus on the lifting sensation inside your body. A correct Kegel feels like drawing a marble upward. Your glutes and thighs should stay relaxed.
